## DeWalt DCST922B (String Trimmer / Weed Eater)
- 20V MAX, 13" cutting swath, ~$99 bare tool
- Trim-only; does NOT cut grass to an even height
- Primary use: edging, weeds, tight spots — fence lines and against house
- Not sufficient as primary mower for any yard > 300 sq ft

## Recommendation Matrix

| Yard size (usable backyard) | Primary tool | Secondary tool | Why |
|---|---|---|---|
| < 800 sq ft | String trimmer alone | — | Live with uneven cut |
| 800–2,500 sq ft | Reel mower (Fiskars StaySharp) | Trimmer for edges/weeds | Best combo: zero gas/cord/battery, cleanest cut sub-$300 |
| 2,500–5,000 sq ft | Small push mower (gas or electric) | Trimmer for edges/weeds | Reel mower becomes slow/awkward |
| > 5,000 sq ft | Self-propelled or riding | Trimmer | Anything larger needs power assist |

## Specific Recommendations

Reel mower (city lot winner):
- Fiskars StaySharp 19" model 362 — yards up to ~2,500 sq ft
- Fiskars StaySharp 17" model 6294 — tighter yards (~1,000 sq ft)
- $100–130 at Lowe's or Amazon
- Zero maintenance, cleanest cut, quiet enough for morning use

Workflow: trim weed-heavy/thick spots first with string trimmer, then reel-mow.

Skip:
- String-trimmer-only (uneven, unprofessional look)
- Reel mowers under 17" (too narrow = too many passes)
- Reel mowers under $80 (cheap gear, plastic components, blades warp)
- Electric mowers under $200 (quality gap — Fiskars beats them until you hit ~$300)
